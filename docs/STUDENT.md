# Student Handbook — Madrasa

Welcome. This portal shows your progress at the madrasa — how your Quran
recitation is improving, which surahs you've memorised, which duas you've
completed, and your attendance. Use it to check what your teacher is tracking,
and to download a printable progress report whenever you need one (e.g. to show
your parents).

---

## 1. Signing in

Your teacher or madrasa admin will give you:

- A **username** (e.g. `aisha.malik`) — sometimes a real email instead.
- A **temporary password**.

Steps:

1. Go to the madrasa website (ask if you don't know the URL).
2. Click **Sign in** at the top right.
3. Type your username (or email) in the Username field.
4. Type your password.
5. **Sign in** — you land on your dashboard.

Once you're in, go to **My Account** at the bottom of the sidebar and set a
password you'll remember.

---

## 2. The sidebar

| Item | What it shows |
|---|---|
| My Dashboard | Quick overview: latest Quran score, memorisation %, surahs complete |
| My Grades | Every Quran Recitation assessment you've ever had |

## 3. My Dashboard

When you sign in, the first thing you see is your dashboard with:

- **Ayahs memorised** — total count across all surahs.
- **% of Quran** — your memorisation as a percentage of all 6236 ayahs.
- **Surahs completed** — how many surahs you've finished fully.
- **Recitation trend chart** — a line showing your last 10 Quran assessment averages.
- **Latest scores table** — each assessment with the five category scores
  (Fluency, Makharij, Tajweed, Waqf, Accuracy) and the overall grade.

If the chart is empty, you haven't had your first assessment yet. Once your
teacher records one, refresh the page.

---

## 4. My Grades

A detailed history of every Quran Recitation assessment your teacher has
recorded. For each one you see:

- The **date**.
- Per-category scores: **F** (Fluency), **M** (Makharij), **T** (Tajweed),
  **W** (Waqf), **A** (Accuracy).
- The **average** of those five scores.
- The **grade band** (Very Weak → Excellent).
- Teacher **comments**, if any.

### What the grade bands mean

| Band | Average | What it usually means |
|---|---|---|
| Not Attempted | 0 | Couldn't read this assessment |
| Very Weak | 0.5-1.4 | Many mistakes; need teacher beside you |
| Weak | 1.5-2.4 | Frequent pauses; Tajweed rarely used |
| Satisfactory | 2.5-3.4 | Getting there; basic Tajweed |
| Good | 3.5-4.4 | Steady; Tajweed in most places |
| Excellent | 4.5-5 | Clear, accurate, Tajweed throughout |

It's normal to start in **Weak** or **Satisfactory** and improve over time.
Don't worry about a low first score — it's the trend that matters.

## 5. Memorisation

The Memorisation screen lists every surah from Al-Fatihah and Juz Amma. For
each one you see:

- **#** — surah number.
- **Surah** — transliterated name (e.g. "Al-Fatihah").
- **Arabic** — the surah name in Arabic script.
- **Progress bar** — how much of this surah you've memorised (e.g. 3 / 7 ayahs).
- **Status** — `not_started`, `in_progress`, or `completed`.
- **Last revised** — when your teacher last checked you on this surah.

The KPIs at the top show **total ayahs memorised**, **% of Quran**, and
**surahs completed**.

If a number looks wrong, ask your teacher to update it during your next
revision session.

---

## 6. Duas

You'll see two sections:

- **Daily Duas** — things to say before eating, after eating, before sleep, etc.
- **Namaz Duas** — things to say during salah: Takbir, Tashahhud, Durood, etc.

For each dua:

- **Title** + **Translation** in English.
- **Arabic** — the dua in Arabic script.
- **Transliteration** — Arabic in Latin letters.
- **Status** chip — pending / in_progress / completed.
- **Score** — your teacher's 0-5 oral mastery score.

The bar at the top of each category shows your overall completion percentage.

---

## 7. Attendance

Shows your attendance history for the last 180 days.

- **Attendance rate** — percentage of days you were present.
- Each row: date, class, status chip (Present / Absent / Late / Excused),
  optional note.

If your attendance looks wrong, tell your teacher.

---

## 8. My Reports

Click **Download PDF** to get a single A4 report card containing:

- Your name and student code.
- Last 10 Quran Recitation assessments with a **Trend** column
  (showing improvement or decline per assessment).
- Memorisation summary.
- Daily + Namaz Duas completion.
- Signature lines for the teacher and principal.

Useful for parents' evening or showing your progress at home.

---

## 9. My Account

1. Sidebar → **My Account**.
2. See your name, role, sign-in identifier, and account ID.
3. Change your password: type a new one twice, click **Update password**.

### Forgot password?

- If your sign-in is a **real email** — click **Forgot password?** on the
  login page, enter your email, and use the link in the email you receive.
- If your sign-in is a **username only** — ask your teacher or the admin to
  reset it for you. There's no email to send a reset link to.

## 10. Common questions

**Why is my Quran score lower than I expected?**
Your teacher scores honestly so the system can track your improvement. Look at
the **Trend** column — what matters is whether your scores are going up over
time, not the absolute number.

**Can I see other students' grades?**
No. The system is set up so each student only sees their own data. Teachers
and admins see more, but they're the only ones.

**Can my parents log in?**
Right now, parents need to use your account or ask the school for their own
parent account (the feature is being rolled out).

**Where can I print a copy?**
**My Reports → Download PDF**, then print the PDF. You'll get a nicely-formatted
A4 report card with the school logo.

**I have a problem with my account.**
Talk to your teacher or the school office. They have admin access and can fix
most things in a minute.

---

## 11. Tips

- **Check My Grades after every lesson.** If a score doesn't look right, ask
  your teacher to recheck — they can re-record it from their side.
- **Set a strong password** — 12+ characters, mix of letters/numbers.
- **Sign out** when you're done if you're on a shared device.
- **Use the Trend column** on the Quran tab to see if you're improving. A
  steady green arrow over several weeks is better than one high score.

Allah grant you success in your studies. May He make the Quran a guide and a
light for you.
